What this measures

Five real barriers: how strict the ID law is, how long people wait at polls, how many households lack internet for online registration, language access, and how many polling places are available relative to the population.

Where the data comes from

ID laws from the National Conference of State Legislatures (2025). Wait times and polling access from MIT's Elections Performance Index (2024 election). Internet and language data from the US Census Bureau's American Community Survey (2024).

Why this exists

The SAVE Act would require a passport or birth certificate to register to vote. Over 21 million Americans don't have easy access to those documents. This map shows the barriers that already exist — before that law even takes effect.
