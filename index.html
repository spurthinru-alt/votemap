What this index measures

The Voting Friction Index assigns every US state a score from 0 to 100 reflecting how many structural barriers stand between a citizen and their vote. Higher scores mean more friction — more obstacles, more cost, more difficulty.

It is built for journalists, researchers, policy advocates, educators, and anyone who wants to understand structural inequality in American elections.

Hover over any state on the map for a full breakdown. Use the buttons above to isolate individual dimensions.


Note: This is a public education tool, not a legal determination. Laws change frequently — always verify your state's current rules at your local election office. Scores marked with * are research estimates pending updated source data.
